How Is Plaster Made?

One of the most curious subjects in the building industry is plastering. Plaster is made to protect the weak wall material and to prevent moisture in general. In addition, the plaster made to correct uneven surfaces is divided into two types as exterior plaster and interior plaster.

How Is Plaster Made?

Plaster is a wall material that is frequently heard in the building industry and it is a material that provides benefits in many ways. How is plaster made? What should be considered when plastering? These questions are asked by many people.

These processes, which vary as rough plaster and final plaster, are applied differently from each other. Rough plastering is done after the preparation of the mortar. It is also very important to clean the area to be applied from foreign matters before starting the plastering process. It is necessary to separate the materials such as dust, sawdust, and rubble and make the surface smooth.

After these processes, it should be ensured that the surface is thoroughly dry. If the surface is not dry, problems such as poor adhesion of the mortar may occur. It is also very important to wet the surface with the help of a brush before applying the mortar.

Thin plaster is called sand mixture in which the mortar is approximately 5 mm. It is generally applied to fill the gaps left after rough plastering or to give the interiors a more beautiful appearance. If the thin plaster is to be used indoors, protective tapes are also used to prevent it from getting on the windows. In addition, it is very important for the surface to be dry and to make the hot surfaces wet in the final plaster process.

What are the Plaster Types?

The plaster used indoors and outdoors in the building industry is divided into two types. These are interior plaster and exterior plaster. Interior plasters are applied to interior areas such as stairs, ceilings and columns. It is applied to these areas before the painting process of the wall and makes it ready for painting. In addition, interior plasters are preferred also for decorative purposes today.

Another type is exterior plaster. Exterior plasters are applied to the exterior surfaces of the buildings. Exterior plaster applied to the surfaces of the buildings is used to prepare the building for coating with materials such as painting and insulation.

How is Plaster Mortar Prepared?

There are mixtures prepared with stucco and mortar in plaster applications. Mixtures made with mortar are prepared as rough and final. Plasters usually have a protective function. Accordingly, the rough plaster mortar is prepared with the following materials:

- 1 measure of cement
- 2 scoops of lime
- 9 scales of sand

In addition, if the final plaster is to be made on the rough plaster, it must be ensured that it is in a thinner structure. Final plaster mortar is prepared with the following materials:

- 1 measure of cement
- 11 scoops of sand
- 2 scoops of powdered lime

How to Fix a Plaster Crack?

In case of plaster cracks, it is absolutely necessary to treat. The material required for this is gypsum. The materials required to prepare gypsum are as follows:

- Plaster
- Wall paint
- Primer
- Trowel
- Paint brush

In order to remove the plaster crack, first of all, plaster and water should be mixed. This mixture should be applied to the crack. Then the surface should be smoothed with a trowel and primer should be applied. After this process, the wall should be left to dry and painted with the same color paint as the wall so that there is no color difference.
