What Is The Language And Use Of Colors?

You see colors in everything around you at every moment of the day, but have you ever thought about the effects each of these colors have on you? Every color has a meaning, whether it's the calming effect of blue skies and green spaces or the hunger-inducing reds and yellows of your local fast food chain. These colors touch the emotions.

There is a whole science (and art) in the meanings of colors. What is the harmony and the language of colors? Which colors are compatible with each other? What are the trending colors this year? If you have such questions and if you want to choose colors wisely, you must first know the meanings of colors. Because colors can be a powerful tool as long as they show their meaning.

Meaning and Warmth Added by Colors to the Environment

The meanings of colors stem from psychological influences, cultural developments and biological conditioning. Some color meanings are deeply rooted in our brains, such as the color red associated with warmth or the color green with nature because these are the colors that appear everywhere and stand out.

Have you ever thought why we avoid vivid colors? In fact, this is a game played by nature. Bright and vibrantly colored animals or plants are often poisonous. This also affects our psychology. On the other hand, we are drawn to red fruit rather than green fruit because the color indicates maturity and sweetness.

It is a proven fact that women who prefer red clothing in an environment attract more attention. Other people find them attractive, dangerous, and mature. That's why they manage to attract everyone to themselves. However, you cannot achieve the same effect in an environment such as a workplace. The use of red color in space paint is tiring on the eyes and triggers distancing over time.

Which Colors Should Be Used on Which Places?

Color holds power. It can affect our mood, emotions, and behavior. It can also be a source of information. While an individual's response to color may stem from personal experience, color psychology along with color science supports the idea that there is much more to it. Numerous studies have been conducted on the relationship between colors, especially in the fields of marketing and branding. E.g;

-Color influences 85% of shoppers' purchasing decisions.
-About 62-90% of product evaluation is based on only colors.
-Colors increase brand popularity by 80%.

At a fundamental level, colors affect how consumers see the 'personality' of the brand, so getting it right is important.

Warm Colors

This includes variations such as red, yellow, orange, and pink. These colors evoke warmth due to their brightness and connection with the sun. They generally carry optimism, enthusiasm, and passion. They are used in open areas and places such as nightclubs.

Cool Colors

These include variations such as green, blue, purple, and violet. These colors are considered cool as they are commonly found in nature and are known for their calming effects. They are the colors used mostly in children's rooms, sometimes in the guest room or bedroom, in the toilet, bathroom and kitchen.

Neutral Colors

These include variations such as brown, black, white, and gray. They are often paired with warm or cool colors, but are sophisticated on their own. They can be strong and pure and are sometimes referred to as earth tones. Cream color is generally living room colors. White color is preferred in clothing stores so that the products will stand out more.

Which Color is Used with Which Color to create Warm and Intimate Spaces?

Keep color psychology in mind when deciding what colors to paint your walls or what color slipcovers to buy. Warm tones have an energizing effect, suitable for the kitchen or living room, while cool tones evoke calm feelings. A bedroom or bathroom can benefit from cool colors. Neutrals are best for formal areas and hallways.
